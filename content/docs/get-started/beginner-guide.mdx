# Beginner Guide

## The simple version

JouleBridge helps a site do three things:

1. understand what assets and limits exist right now
2. make local decisions safely
3. leave behind a signed record of what happened

## Why that matters

Distributed energy sites are hard to operate because chargers, batteries, solar, meters, and local constraints do not arrive as one clean system.

Even when a cloud tool exists, the site still needs a local runtime that can:

- keep operating if connectivity degrades
- respect site-specific limits
- explain actions later to operators, support, finance, or counterparties

## The runtime split

### Bridge Kernel

Runs near the equipment and the local interfaces.

It turns policy, constraints, and intent into feasible local actions, then keeps the signed site record close to the equipment.

### JouleBridge Cloud

Distributes policy, receives proofs, and helps operators understand many sites at once.

### JouleBridge Console

Exposes the operator and support view.

## The initial commercial wedges

The first places this matters commercially are industrial sites and EV fleet depots.

The broader platform story stays the same, but the first repeatable deployment path starts there.
